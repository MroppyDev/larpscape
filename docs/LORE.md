# THE CANTORNE CODEX
### The Canonical Lore Bible of Larpscape

> This document is binding canon. Every quest, dialogue line, examine text, wiki page, and
> item description written after this file exists must be consistent with it. Where existing
> in-game text (quests, dialogue in `src/packs/`, examines in `data/*.json`) states a fact,
> this bible incorporates and extends it — it never contradicts it. NPC ids, item ids, and
> place names below match the live data files exactly.

---

## 1. Cosmology: The World That Was Sung

The world is called **Cantorne** (kan-TORN). It was not forged, and it was not dreamt.
It was *sung*.

Before anything, there was the **Choir of Five** — five Voices without throats, singing in
the **Singing Dark**. Their song is called the **First Chord**, and everything that exists
is a held note of it:

- **Aulden, the Deep Voice** — stone, mountains, ore, patience. Miners say every vein of
  metal is a bar of Aulden's bass line, and smiths say an anvil rings true because it
  remembers the tune.
- **Brell, the Rolling Voice** — water, tide, rivers, rain, weather. Sailors call a calm sea
  "Brell holding her breath."
- **Syla, the Green Voice** — growth, beasts, forests, harvests, rot-that-feeds-roots. Every
  seed is a grace note of Syla's.
- **Imber, the Bright Voice** — fire, light, heat, the sun, ambition. Forges, hearths, and
  bad ideas all burn with borrowed Imber.
- **Quiess, the Thin Voice** — air, breath, silence between notes, and the dead. Quiess
  carries the souls of the dead back into the Chord, which is why prayers are *hummed*, not
  spoken: Quiess only listens to music.

**The Flaw.** At the final cadence of the First Chord, something slipped in that the Five
never sang: a sixth sound, sour and off-rhythm, called **the Offnote**. The Offnote is not a
god and not a devil; it is a *mistake that wants to be repeated*. Wherever the world's song
goes out of tune — battlefields, abandoned places, stagnant water, untended graves, greed
left to simmer — the Offnote pools like cold grease, and things congeal out of it or are
warped by it. Every monster in Cantorne is, at root, a place where the song skipped:
goblins are a marching-chant gone feral, wraiths are notes that refused to return to
Quiess, the Bog Horror is a whole drowned refrain. This is canon and downstream writers
should use it: **monsters are music gone wrong, and killing them is retuning the world.**

The Five still sing — quietly, underneath everything. Mortals who learn to hum along call
it Prayer. Mortals who learn the song's *grammar* call it Magic. Mortals who chip frozen
syllables of it out of rock call it Runecraft. Mortals who fill a brass casing with
powdered thunder and pull a trigger call it Tuesday in Aldgate.

## 2. The Calendar and the Four Eras

Cantorne counts years **F.S. — "from the Settling"** — dated from the year the first
mortal villages woke in the Bellmeadow vale. A year has twelve months called **bars**, each
of thirty days, plus five intercalary **Rest Days** at midwinter when, by ancient custom,
no contracts may be signed and no wars declared (the casino remains open; Pip checked the
wording). **The present year is F.S. 743.**

### Era I — The Unstruck Age (before counting)
The Choir sings the First Chord. Land, sea, beast, and the bones of every mountain are laid
down. The Offnote slips into the cadence; its largest fragments fall to earth — one into
the southern peatlands (the future **Deep Bog**), one into the fire-veins beneath the
eastern caverns (the future **Ashen Depths**), and countless slivers everywhere else. No
mortals yet. The era ends when the Five lower their voices to a hum and the first people
wake mid-meadow with the melody stuck in their heads.

### Era II — The High Stave (F.S. 1 – 612)
The classical age: kingdoms, choirs, trade, towers.
- **F.S. 1** — *The Settling.* First villages in the Bellmeadow vale on the River Murmur.
- **F.S. 88–94** — **Stonecourt Castle** raised by Duke Coram the Mason, first of the
  Stonecourt line, to ward the vale.
- **F.S. 211** — In the then-green south, the singing city of **Sarrash** is founded; its
  thousand-throat choirs keep the land in tune and in bloom.
- **F.S. 305** — Aldgate chartered as a market town at the old toll-arch ("the Ald Gate")
  on the east road.
- **F.S. 388** — **Maraza** crowned Queen of the Frostpeak holds, greatest singer of her
  age.
- **F.S. 402** — *Maraza's Solo.* Convinced she could sing a Verse of the First Chord
  alone, Maraza attempts it at the summit. The note holds her, her court, and the high
  passes in ice mid-syllable. She has been furious about it for 341 years. (Her examine
  text — "frozen mid-fury, several centuries ago, still furious" — is literal.)
- **F.S. 537** — Sarrash falls silent in a single night (the cause is a guarded mystery;
  the Stillwater Circle believes its choirs were lured into singing the Offnote's
  counter-melody). Without song, the green south dries to the **Sunscorch Desert** within a
  generation; the sand swallows the city, leaving the **Sunken Ruins** and the wraiths that
  remember roofs.

### Era III — The Cracked Refrain (F.S. 612 – 701)
The collapse. On the **Night of Skipped Beats** (F.S. 612), the Offnote fragment under the
southern peat surges; the bog drowns three villages and exhales decades of war. Goblin
warbands — feral marching-chants given legs — pour out of every untuned corner.
- **F.S. 612–690** — The **Discord Wars**: the Duchy, Aldgate, the Frostpeak holds, and
  the ports fight goblin hosts, bandit kings, and each other. The Underdeep is breached and
  abandoned twice. Brackwater burns once and learns to build in stone.
- **F.S. 701** — The last goblin host of the wars breaks at the gates of Aldgate. The era
  closes.

### Era IV — The Quiet Measure (F.S. 701 – present, 743)
The current age: recovery, commerce, eccentricity, and a low hum of returning trouble.
- **F.S. 703** — Duke Reginald the Odd of the Southern Lawn dies childless and leaves his
  entire demesne, by airtight will, to his beloved performing chimpanzee. The courts uphold
  it. **Danquavious Chimperton I** takes the throne of the Southern Lawn; his line has
  ruled — competently, by most measures — ever since.
- **F.S. 712** — Quarrymen in the Ashen Depths spoil-heaps discover **thunder-shale**:
  brittle black stone that holds a splinter of the Offnote's percussion. Milled fine, it
  becomes **gunpowder**.
- **F.S. 718** — The **Aldgate Gun Guild** chartered after Armsmith Hannelore Glock's
  eighteenth pistol pattern finally fails to explode (patterns one through seventeen are
  commemorated on a scorched plaque). The **Glock 18** becomes the realm's standard
  sidearm.
- **F.S. 726** — The Aldgate Exchange opens its gilded booths; coin learns to move faster
  than carts.
- **F.S. 731** — Dealer Pip opens **Pip's House of Mild Regret** in Bellmeadow's south
  quarter.
- **F.S. 740** — The Deep Bog stirs again. Bog ticks carrying discord-motes spread north.
- **F.S. 741** — Dr. Ticksworth opens the **Dentist Tick Eat** clinic; properly prepared
  tick consumption is found to neutralize discord-motes. Glen volunteers. Glen keeps
  volunteering.
- **F.S. 742** — The present crisis year: a goblin warlord seizes the old palisade fort
  east of Aldgate; a horror congeals in the Deep Bog; the Shadow Drake nests in the
  Underdeep; hammering is heard from the deepest Ashen Depths as **Korr the Molten** wakes;
  Saif the Red Smile strangles the desert caravan roads; and Stonecourt begins licensing
  **adventurers**.
- **F.S. 743** — Now. You arrive.

## 3. Regions and Places

### Bellmeadow and Stonecourt Castle (`castle` POI — "The Castle")
**Bellmeadow** is the starter town: the oldest continuously settled vale in Cantorne, on
the west bank of the River Murmur. **Stonecourt Castle** (the duchy seat, music:
*Stonecourt*) has warded it since F.S. 94. The current ruler is **Duke Aldous Stonecourt**,
a decent administrator with a sweet tooth (Cook Edda confirms he had three slices at the
banquet; his physician is not to be told). The castle houses the chapel altar, Edda's
kitchen, and the duchy's records. Politics: the Duke licenses adventurers, funds Brogan's
slayer ledger, and pretends the casino and the chimp court are someone
else's jurisdiction (they are: see Factions). Hooks: the duchy archive holds the only
surviving Discord Wars maps of the Underdeep; the larder is chronically under-provisioned.

### Bellmeadow South District (Chimperton court, Tick Eat clinic)
Two institutions share the lawn south of the castle, and both are deadly serious:
- **The Court of the Southern Lawn** — seat of **Danquavious Chimperton III, Sovereign of
  Bananas, Duke of the Southern Lawn**, third chimp of his line since the Will of F.S. 703.
  The court is legally unimpeachable, fiscally solvent, and the only monarchy in Cantorne
  to win both a joust and a banana-eating contest. The golden banana on its pedestal has
  never been peeled and, legend insists, never will be. Do not ask about the First or the
  Second (the First died beloved; the Second abdicated to pursue percussion).
- **The Dentist Tick Eat clinic** — Dr. Ticksworth's practice (est. F.S. 741): dentistry
  in front, discord-mote remediation in back. Bog ticks carry slivers of the Offnote;
  eaten after proper preparation, the mote is neutralized by stomach acid and spite. Glen
  handles the tasting menu. It is artisanal. It is, technically, public health.

### The Casino (Pip's House of Mild Regret, Bellmeadow's south quarter)
**Pip's House of Mild Regret** — slots, blackjack, roulette, coinflip; "all fair, all
loud"; the house wins slightly more often, which is the definition of fair. Pip's charter
was granted under Rest Day law, which is why it never closes. Pip once won the Aldgate city
seal at cards from the last lord-mayor and returned it framed — which is how Aldgate ended
up governed by a merchant council. The house out-earns the Aldgate Exchange on feast days,
a fact the Concord of Weights quietly resents.

### Aldgate (`aldgate` POI)
The eastern walled city (music: *Aldgate Streets*): the Exchange plaza with its gilded
booths, Hetta's armoury, Pim's grocery, the inn, the city watch, and the **Gun Guild**
hall. Aldgate is run by the **Concord of Weights** — a merchant council that took over when
the last lord-mayor lost the city seal at cards (to Pip, who returned it framed). Politics:
Aldgate wants the warlord's fort cleared because the fort sits on its trade road, and pays
adventurers accordingly. Hooks: thunder-shale supply runs through Aldgate; whoever controls
the Depths road controls gunpowder.

### Warlord's Fort (`warlords_fort` POI)
A Cracked Refrain-era palisade fort east of Aldgate (music: *Warbanner*), seized in F.S.
742 by the **Goblin Warlord** and his Broken-Tooth banner. The banner is load-bearing —
goblins are a marching-chant made flesh, and the banner *is* the chant's notation; tear it
down and the warband forgets its own rhythm. This is why every warband collapses when its
warlord falls.

### The River Murmur (`river` POI)
The vale's river (music: *Riverside*), named because it audibly hums Brell's line of the
First Chord on still mornings. Willows, fishing spots, the old bridge. Custom: oaths sworn
on the bridge are considered witnessed.

### Hunter Meadow (`hunter_meadow` POI)
Flower meadow south-east of the vale (music: *Quiet Meadow*), tuned so gently that snared
birds are said to be merely embarrassed rather than afraid. Trappers' common land under
duchy law, walked by snare-setters who hold its quiet as carefully as any tuned instrument.

### Swamp Mine (`swamp_mine` POI)
The duchy's old ore mine, half-swallowed by the bog's slow northward creep (music:
*Boghollow*). Still worked — copper, tin, iron, coal, and rune essence — because Aulden's
bass line runs shallow here. The cave mouth at its south end is the only safe descent
toward the Underdeep. Miners knock twice on the timbers going in: once for Aulden, once
for whoever didn't come back in '88.

### Deep Bog (`deep_bog` POI)
Where the first Offnote fragment fell. The drowned refrain of three villages lost on the
Night of Skipped Beats still circulates in the peat, and in F.S. 742 it congealed a body:
the **Bog Horror**. The **Stillwater Circle** (see Factions) tends the bog's edges,
harvesting the strangely potent herbs that grow where the song decomposes. Hooks: the bog
heart is not the fragment itself — the fragment is still down there.

### The Underdeep (`underdeep` POI)
The great cavern system east and below (music: *Underdeep*): stalagmites, lava pools,
mithril and adamantite. Breached and abandoned twice in the Discord Wars; currently the
nesting ground of the **Shadow Drake**, "the colour of a bad night." The drake's scales
shed darkness the way hot iron sheds sparks.

### Ashen Depths (`ashen_depths` POI)
The deepest, hottest reach of the cavern system (music: *Ashfall*): gold, runite,
thunder-shale spoil heaps, magma crawlers, ash fiends — and at the very bottom, **Korr the
Molten**, the Offnote's second great fragment wearing a body of slag. "Stone that walks.
Fire that thinks" (Brogan, canon). The hammering heard at night in the upper caves is Korr
forging. Nobody knows what.

### Frostpeak Mountains (`frostpeak` POI)
The eastern range (music: *Rimewind*): foothill yews and maples, the mountain agility
course, ice trolls and ice wolves, and at the summit the frozen court of **Maraza the
Rimebound**, held mid-syllable since F.S. 402. The cold radiating from her unfinished note
is why the high passes never thaw. Guide Torvald maintains the routes and a personal
census of every ledge he has fallen off (all of them, once each).

### Sunscorch Desert (`sunscorch_legacy` POI)
Once the green hinterland of singing Sarrash; desert since the city fell silent in F.S.
537 (music: *Sunscorch*). Holds the Sunken Ruins, the gem rocks, the fire altar (a Sarrash
choir-stone that still takes the verse), Zahra's nomad tent, and — since the caravan roads
got profitable again — **Saif the Red Smile's** bandit camp. Saif styles himself King of
the Dunes by vote; he counted the votes himself.

### Port Brackwater (`port_brackwater` POI)
The south-coast port (music: *Brackwater Tide*): docks, warehouses, lobster pots, harpoon
spots, Pell's fish stall, Quill's harbour office. Burned once in the Discord Wars; rebuilt
in stone and stubbornness. Its civic religion is **Tidefest**, the annual coastal catch
competition — Brackwater has entered a shark every year for forty years and regards this
as a load-bearing fact about reality. The Brackstrait ferry (Wick's, when it floats)
serves the western water.

### Future / currently unreachable regions (canon now, content later)
These exist in the world and in disabled packs (`hub_eldermere`, `hub_stonewatch`,
`hub_gullswreck`); their quests and characters are canon and must remain consistent:
- **Eldermere** — farm-belt village beyond the vale, governed by Elder Maeryn's word and
  memory. Its west toll path into Tanglewood chokes with bramble and spiders whenever
  traffic lapses ("Tanglewood remembers every footfall" — Maeryn, canon).
- **The Tanglewood** — the great forest (music: *Tanglewood*, composed and waiting). Syla's
  densest verse; yews, magic trees, mushroom glades, a dark heart clearing where the trees
  grow in a circle and do not discuss why. Cart-wheel-sized spiders at the edges.
- **Stonewatch** — the northern range outpost: a cold garrison, Trapper Hode's fur trade,
  and the duty of watching the passes Maraza's winter still leaks through.
- **Gullswreck Cove** — pirate isle off the west coast, no land route, ferry only. Home of
  the **Wreckers** under Captain Saltjaw, who salvage what the sea takes and take what the
  sea hasn't gotten around to. The wreck of the *Gull* — the treasure ship that named the
  cove — has never been found; Saltjaw carries the chart.
- **Mirrormere** — the great central lake, fed by the Brackstrait. So still it reflects
  the sky a half-second late; folklore says it's the world's tuning fork, and Brell checks
  her pitch in it.
- **The Eastern Marshes** — reed country, herbs, waterfowl, and the politest leeches in
  Cantorne. The Stillwater Circle's mother-lodge stands here on stilts.
- **The Southern Savanna** — open grass beyond the desert: lion-coloured light, scattered
  herds, wayfarer routes. Old Sarrash's pasture lands, slowly going green again as the
  world retunes.
- **Cinderholm** — volcanic isle offshore SE; cinder imps, a causeway at low tide, and a
  dormant cone that the imps treat as a parent. Imber's most concentrated verse above
  ground.

## 4. Factions

**The Duchy of Stonecourt.** Duke Aldous's government: castle, watch, mine charters,
adventurer licenses. Goal: keep the vale fed and the Quiet Measure quiet. Tension: it is
paying adventurers to solve problems its own army (downsized in the long peace) cannot,
and it owes the Concord money.

**The Concord of Weights (Aldgate).** The merchant council: Exchange, city watch payroll,
shop charters, the Gun Guild's parent body. Goal: open roads, moving coin. Tension: wants
the fort cleared and the Depths road safe for thunder-shale; quietly resents that
Bellmeadow's casino out-earns its Exchange on feast days.

**The Aldgate Gun Guild.** Chartered F.S. 718. Keepers of the gunpowder secret (milled
thunder-shale — powdered Offnote percussion, which is why a gunshot sounds like the world
skipping a beat). Doctrine: the Offnote put thunder in the rock to cause trouble, so firing
it *at monsters* is recycling. Master Flint runs the armoury; Sergeant Vex trains recruits.
Every adventurer starts with a Glock 18 — patterns 1–17 are on the memorial plaque.

**The Broken-Tooth Warbands (goblins).** Not a culture but a chant: goblin bands coalesce
around banners, and the banner is the warband's memory. Goal: march, take, chant louder.
Tension: the current Warlord is the first since F.S. 701 to hold a *fort* — which means
some banner survived the wars that shouldn't have.

**The Stillwater Circle.** Herbalists, bog-tenders, and quiet listeners (Old Fen is their
vale-side friend; the mother-lodge is in the Eastern Marshes). Goal: keep the Offnote
fragments *asleep* — they hold that the fragments cannot be destroyed, only out-sung.
Tension: they opposed thunder-shale mining loudly and were ignored; they are not saying
"we told you so," they are brewing it.

**The Court of the Southern Lawn.** Chimperton III's micro-monarchy. Goal: dignity,
bananas, the long defense of the Will of F.S. 703 against jealous human cousins of Duke
Reginald who still file annual appeals. The court keeps the lawn south of the castle and is
the casino's largest single depositor. Herald Bananrick handles volume.

**Saif's Red Smiles / Saltjaw's Wreckers (the criminal coasts).** The desert bandits and
the cove pirates trade through fences in both ports and despise each other ("sand thieves"
vs "wet thieves"). Both kings are charismatic, neither is elected, and both drop their
regalia when finally brought to account.

## 5. Bestiary — origins consistent with the cosmology, drops explained

Every hostile creature is a place where the song skipped. Bones drop because the body was
real even if the cause wasn't; the Offnote builds with borrowed material.

- **Goblin** (`goblin`) — a war-chant from the Discord Wars that never stopped marching;
  spawns wherever old battle-rhythm soaked into the ground. Drops bones, looted coins, and
  the bronze swords they scavenge because they cannot forge, only take.
- **Goblin Warlord** (`goblin_warlord`, boss) — a chant grown a *conductor*. His banner is
  load-bearing canon: it is the warband's sheet music. Drops the banner (the proof Aldgate
  pays for), his looted `warlord_helm` and mithril blade — goblins steal upward.
- **Giant rat** (`giant_rat`) — ordinary vermin grown fat on grain stores and discord-mote
  runoff. Drops what it was: meat and bones.
- **Scorpion** (`scorpion`) — Sunscorch native; "all armour, attitude, and arithmetic of
  stings." Not Offnote work — just the desert's honest opinion of visitors.
- **Bear / Dire wolf / Forest spider / Ice wolf** (`bear`, `dire_wolf`, `forest_spider`,
  `ice_wolf`) — Syla's beasts, swollen where the Tanglewood and Frostpeak verses run loud.
  Drop fur, silk, meat, bones: the forest economy, claws included. The dire wolf grew too
  big for its forest and too hungry for yours (canon examine).
- **Ice troll** (`ice_troll`) — boulders that Maraza's unfinished note taught to have
  opinions. Drop ore, coal, the occasional sapphire: they *are* mineral.
- **Desert bandit** (`desert_bandit`) — human, no excuse. Drops coins and uncut gems
  skimmed from caravans; pickpocketable because Saif teaches greed, not discipline.
- **Magma crawler** (`magma_crawler`) — "a many-legged ember that never learned to cool";
  spillage from Korr's forge given legs. Drops gold ore, coal, fire runes — forge slag.
- **Ash fiend** (`ash_fiend`) — a scorched silhouette: the Depths' dead miners' silhouettes
  baked into the rock and peeled off by Korr's hammering. Drops chaos runes, runite ore,
  rubies, irit — the deep's treasury, carried by its ghosts.
- **Cinder imp** (`cinder_imp`) — Imber's verse over-concentrated on Cinderholm: pocket
  arsonists with ember giggles. Drop fire runes and ember crystals: they sweat fuel.
- **Ruin wraith** (`ruin_wraith`) — Sarrash's choristers, notes that refused to return to
  Quiess when the city fell silent. Drop grave dust (their own residue, prized in
  herblore), big bones, and ranarr — the prayer-herb grows where prayer is owed.
- **Pirate** (`pirate`) — Wreckers of the cove; everything they own belonged to someone
  else, drops included (coins, gems, fishing bait).
- **Bog Horror** (`bog_horror`, boss) — the drowned refrain of three villages, congealed
  F.S. 742; "a heap of swamp that decided to hold grudges." Drops horror hide, the bog
  heart (a clot of the refrain, not the fragment itself), herbs and seeds — the bog gives
  back what it swallowed, slightly fermented.
- **Shadow Drake** (`shadow_drake`, boss) — hatched in the Underdeep dark from an egg laid
  in the wars; its scales shed darkness like sparks. Drops drake scales (always — it
  moults), the drake sword (a blade quenched in its blood holds an edge of night), ember
  crystal, deep ores.
- **Maraza the Rimebound / Ice Queen** (`ice_queen`, boss) — see her Solo, F.S. 402. Not
  Offnote: hubris, preserved. Drops the `rimeglass_blade` (her note, frozen sharp), runite,
  sapphires, prayer potions — a queen's grave goods, distributed posthumously.
- **Saif the Red Smile / Bandit King** (`bandit_king`, boss) — the desert's self-elected
  king. Drops a fortune in coins and gems and his `red_sash` — the smile, surrendered.
- **Korr the Molten / Magma fiend** (`magma_fiend`, boss) — the Offnote's second fragment
  in a body of slag; "the deep speaks in fire, and Korr is its loudest word" (canon).
  Drops the `molten_core` (its heart, which cools but never quite goes cold), molten
  gauntlets, rune-grade metal: Korr forges, and you inherit the workshop.
- **Captain Saltjaw** (`pirate_captain`, boss) — master of the Wreckers; his grin has more
  gold than teeth. Drops the `wreck_chart` (the *Gull*'s resting place — always, it's
  chained to his belt), the boarding cutlass, rubies, swordfish.
- **Chicken / Cow / Sheep** (`chicken`, `cow`, `sheep`) — Syla's gifts to the vale,
  perfectly in tune, completely unbothered. The cow converts grass into beef (canon).

## 7. The 24 Skills, as Cantorne Understands Them

- **Attack** — the discipline of the opening note: where a blade lands is a matter of timing, and timing is music. Swordmasters call a perfect strike "on the beat."
- **Strength** — Aulden's gift carried in the body; the held bass note under every swing. Strongfolk traditionally hum when lifting. It does not help. It is traditional.
- **Defence** — learning the rhythm of an enemy well enough to stand in its rests. Armour helps; knowing when the cymbal crashes helps more.
- **Hitpoints** — the volume of your personal verse. The louder the Choir sings you, the more of you there is to dent.
- **Ranged** — Quiess's art: a loosed arrow rides the breath of the world. Fletchers whisper to shafts; good ones whisper back.
- **Prayer** — humming along with the Five over buried bones, returning stray notes to the Chord. Burying bones is liturgy; the altar just keeps the pitch.
- **Magic** — speaking the song's grammar aloud, with runes as punctuation. Mira's first lesson: the world is a sentence, and you are allowed to edit.
- **Runecraft** — pressing rune essence (blank, unsung stone) against an altar until one syllable of the First Chord freezes into it. The Sarrash fire altar still takes the verse.
- **Slayer** — Brogan's ledger-craft: the science of *which* skipped note a monster is, and what retunes it fastest. Half scholarship, half grudges.
- **Agility** — moving in rhythm with terrain that has no intention of cooperating. Torvald teaches it as falling, postponed.
- **Herblore** — the Stillwater Circle's craft: plants that grow where the song decomposes hold concentrated phrasing; grinding and steeping replays it into a body.
- **Thieving** — the silent bar in anyone's music. The Concord outlaws it, employs it, and audits it, in that order.
- **Crafting** — convincing raw matter that it always wanted to be a ring. Gold takes persuasion well; gems arrive pre-convinced.
- **Fletching** — the patient unsinging of a branch into flight. The bowstring's twang is the only note Quiess will sing for free.
- **Smithing** — duetting with Aulden at an anvil. Every true smith's hammer pattern is unique, registered with no one, and recognized by every other smith within earshot.
- **Mining** — reading Aulden's score in the rock and excusing yourself into it. Knock twice going in.
- **Fishing** — negotiation with Brell, conducted through string, net, and optimism. Tidefest is its high holy day.
- **Cooking** — Imber's domestic verse. Edda's doctrine: a burnt fish is a note held too long, and the range forgives nothing.
- **Firemaking** — borrowing Imber retail. The tinderbox spark is the world's smallest, most reliable miracle, and adventurers leave a trail of them.
- **Woodcutting** — taking timber with respect, because the Tanglewood remembers every footfall (Maeryn, canon) and the willows gossip downstream.
- **Farming** — planting Syla's grace notes and keeping the goblins off the seedbeds. Old Fen holds that a well-turned patch is a tuned instrument.
- **Construction** — Aulden's craft scaled to mortal patience: planks, nails, and the stubborn belief that shelter is a chord you can stand inside.
- **Hunter** — Syla's quieter conversation: snares, sign, and meadow patience. Hunter Meadow is tuned so gently that caught birds are merely embarrassed.
- **Gun** — the newest skill (F.S. 712): milled thunder-shale — powdered Offnote percussion — fired through guild-pattern pistols. The Guild's doctrine is recycling: the Offnote put the thunder in the rock to cause trouble, so aim it at trouble. Trained, like Ranged, by hitting things; the Glock 18 is every adventurer's first issue, and patterns 1–17 are on the plaque.

## 8. The Player's Place: Adventurers

An **adventurer** is a person in whom the world's song runs unusually loud — loud enough
that the Choir notices when it stops. This is the canonical explanation for respawning:
when an adventurer falls, the Five hum the verse back a few bars, and the adventurer wakes
at their last anchored place, lighter a few possessions (the Offnote keeps a cut; it
always keeps a cut). Ordinary folk do not get this. They know it, you know it, and the
polite thing is not to mention it.

Since F.S. 742 the Duchy of Stonecourt issues **adventurer licenses** — a war-measure
without a war, because the Quiet Measure has stopped being quiet: the fort, the bog, the
drake, the hammering. Adventurers are freelancers of the retuning: they kill what skipped,
carry what's needed, and get paid by whoever's problem it was. Bankers serve them
identically everywhere (Vaultwright oath), shopkeepers tolerate them, Brogan assigns them,
the Exchange profits from them, and every faction in Section 4 has work for them. The old
soldiers of the duchy regard adventurers with professional respect and mild concern:
someone has to walk toward the trouble so the rest never have to again.

## 9. Appendix: Canon Facts Quick Reference

Single-line facts. Downstream writers may quote freely; contradicting them is a bug.

1. The world is named **Cantorne** and was sung into being by the **Choir of Five**.
2. The Five Voices: **Aulden** (stone), **Brell** (water), **Syla** (growth), **Imber** (fire), **Quiess** (air, breath, the dead).
3. **The Offnote** is the flaw in the First Chord; all monsters are music gone wrong.
4. The Offnote cannot be destroyed, only out-sung or starved — Stillwater Circle doctrine.
5. Years are counted **F.S.** ("from the Settling"); the present year is **F.S. 743**.
6. A year is twelve 30-day **bars** plus five midwinter **Rest Days** (no contracts, no wars; casino exempt by charter wording).
7. The four eras: **Unstruck Age**, **High Stave** (F.S. 1–612), **Cracked Refrain** (612–701), **Quiet Measure** (701–now).
8. The starter town is **Bellmeadow**, in the vale of the **River Murmur**.
9. The Castle is **Stonecourt Castle**, seat of **Duke Aldous Stonecourt**; the duke had three slices of banquet cake and his physician must not be told.
10. The Discord Wars began on the **Night of Skipped Beats**, F.S. 612, when the Deep Bog's Offnote fragment surged.
11. **Danquavious Chimperton III** rules the Southern Lawn legally, via Duke Reginald the Odd's airtight will of F.S. 703.
14. Do not ask about Chimperton the First (died beloved) or the Second (abdicated for percussion).
15. The court's **golden banana** has never been peeled and never will be.
16. **Gunpowder** is milled **thunder-shale** from the Ashen Depths — powdered Offnote percussion — discovered F.S. 712.
17. The **Aldgate Gun Guild** (chartered F.S. 718) holds that shooting monsters with the Offnote's own thunder is recycling.
18. The **Glock 18** is Armsmith Hannelore Glock's eighteenth pattern; patterns 1–17 exploded and are on a memorial plaque.
19. Every adventurer starts with a Glock 18 (Sergeant Vex, canon).
20. Aldgate is governed by the **Concord of Weights**, a merchant council; the last lord-mayor lost the city seal at cards to Pip, who returned it framed.
21. Exchange clerks may not own what they list (Concord neutrality oath).
22. Goblins are a war-chant made flesh; a warband's **banner is its sheet music**, which is why the Warlord's banner is load-bearing.
23. The **Bog Horror** is the drowned refrain of three villages lost on the Night of Skipped Beats.
24. The Deep Bog's Offnote fragment is still down there; the bog heart is only a clot of it.
25. **Maraza the Rimebound** froze herself mid-Solo in F.S. 402 trying to sing a Verse alone; her unfinished note is why the high passes never thaw.
26. **Korr the Molten** is the Offnote's second great fragment; the night hammering in the Depths is Korr forging (Brogan's worst theory: a bell).
27. **Sarrash**, the singing city, fell silent in F.S. 537; its hinterland dried into the Sunscorch Desert and its choristers became the ruin wraiths.
28. Ruin wraiths want the final hymn sung so they can return to Quiess; slaying them is a mercy.
29. **Saif the Red Smile** is King of the Dunes by vote; he counted the votes himself; his red sash is Sarrash temple silk.
30. Port Brackwater has entered a shark in **Tidefest** every year for forty years.
31. **Captain Saltjaw** carries the chart to the wreck of the *Gull* and has never dared dive it.
32. Gullswreck Cove has no land route — ferry only, via Boatman Wick.
33. Wick's ferry lists worse than his last three marriages.
34. **Pip's House of Mild Regret**: the house wins slightly more often — that's the definition of fair.
35. The **Dentist Tick Eat** clinic (F.S. 741) neutralizes discord-mote ticks via proper preparation and consumption; it is artisanal and, technically, public health.
38. Dr. Ticksworth has never eaten a tick; Glen has eaten more of the Offnote than any mortal in history, to no effect except mild smugness.
39. Banks are identical everywhere because the **Vaultwrights** keep tuned stasis-vaults under one realm-wide oath.

50. Music-track names (Stonecourt, Boghollow, Rimewind, Sunscorch, Brackwater Tide, Ashfall, etc.) are in-world names used by the people of those places.
